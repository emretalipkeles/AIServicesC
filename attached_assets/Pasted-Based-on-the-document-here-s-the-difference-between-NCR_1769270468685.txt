Based on the document, here's the difference between NCRs and IDRs and how your AI should handle them:
Inspector Daily Reports (IDRs)
What they are:

Daily field observations written by inspectors
Capture what's happening on site day-to-day
Starting around July 2025, inspectors flag potential contractor delays with code "CODE_CIE"
Broader scope - includes observations, progress notes, incidents, problems

Characteristics:

Volume: High - daily reports across entire project
Specificity: Variable - some days have clear delay incidents, others are routine observations
Delay indicator: Inspector's judgment call using CODE_CIE flag
Causation: May require interpretation (inspector suspects contractor issue)

Example IDR entry:
"Paving crew arrived 2 hours late due to equipment breakdown. CODE_CIE - Activity 1.2.3"

Non-Conformance Reports (NCRs)
What they are:

Formal documentation of quality failures or work that doesn't meet specifications
Triggers mandatory rework or corrective action
Referenced in the scope as "Poor workmanship requiring rework that is already or can be an NCR"

Characteristics:

Volume: Lower - only issued when work fails inspection
Specificity: High - documents specific defect with corrective action required
Delay indicator: Almost certain delay (rework takes time)
Causation: Definitively contractor-caused (quality failure)

Example NCR:
"NCR-045: Curb ramp does not meet ADA slope requirements. Contractor to remove and rebuild per specification."

How Your AI Should Treat Them Differently
For IDRs (CODE_CIE entries):

Parse for incident narratives

Look for descriptions of problems, delays, issues
Extract: what happened, when, why work stopped/slowed


Confidence scoring is critical

Inspector observations may be subjective
AI needs to assess: "Is this really a delay event?"
Some CODE_CIE entries might be false positives


Estimate delay impact

IDRs often describe incidents without stating exact delay duration
AI needs to infer: "Equipment breakdown = X hours delay"


Verify responsibility

Just because inspector flagged CODE_CIE doesn't guarantee it's contractor-caused
AI should analyze the narrative to confirm responsibility

For NCRs:

Treat as definitive delay events

NCR = work failed = rework required = delay
Higher confidence that delay occurred


Extract rework scope

What failed inspection?
What corrective action is required?
This helps estimate delay duration


Delay duration estimation

Rework time = removal + redo + re-inspection
More predictable than incident-based delays


Responsibility is clearer

NCRs are issued for quality failures
Almost always contractor-caused (unless it's design defect)
